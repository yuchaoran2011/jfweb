Russian Tanks near border of Crimea

Source: Daily Times, September 2014

According to Pentagon officials, Russia has increased its military presence in Crimea. It seems that the most modern Russian battle tanks will be stationed at a new air base established near the Crimean border.

The amount of Russian weapons and equipment that is brought into the air base reveals Russia’s plan to use this airfield as a center for military supplies to support further operations in Crimea.

Based on satellite footage, airfield south of Krasnos holds the following: approximately half a dozen Mark IX tanks, 15 howitzers, 35 armored personnel carriers, 200 marines and capacity for approximately 1,500 members of personnel.

Russian foreign minister Sergey V. Lavrov said that Russia is providing Crimea with military supplies and it is logical that such shipments are accompanied by appropriate military specialists.

Since Russia will continue to defend its annexation of Crimea, it inevitably causes further tension in Russia’s relations with the United States.
