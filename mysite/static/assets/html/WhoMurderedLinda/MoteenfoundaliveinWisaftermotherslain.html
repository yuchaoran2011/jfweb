Mo. teen found alive in Wis. after mother slain

Source: Newsweek

By CRIMESIDER STAFF, June 17, 2016, 1:17 PM

SPRINGFIELD, Mo. -- A Missouri teen has been found after the teen's mother was found murdered in their Springfield home, reports CBS affiliate KOLR.

Tracy Nelson, 19, was found alive in Wis. early Thursday, officials said in a news release Thursday afternoon. She was reported missing after her mother Linda Nelson was found slain early Monday in their home, reports the station.

Linda Nelsonâ€™s death was of a "violent nature," Greene County Sheriff Jim Arnott said Thursday. He reportedly wouldn't detail her cause of death.

Authorities responded to the home after friends reportedly called police, saying they saw a disturbing post on the mother's Facebook page indicating she may have been harmed. A Facebook page that appears to belong to Nelson contains a message posted around 3:30 p.m. Sunday that reads, "That b---h is dead!"

Deputy Cathy Ussery with the Greene County Sheriff's Office told 48 Hours Crimesider investigators are looking into the Facebook post and trying to determine who posted it -- whether it may have been one of the two women, another person connected to the investigation or someone who hacked into the account.

Officers issued an "endangered persons advisory" for daughter Tracy Nelson before she was found with a young man, in the Village of Big Bend, Wis. -- nearly 600 miles from her home. She reportedly has Muscular Dystrophy and uses a wheelchair.

Neighbors told the station they last saw Tracy Nelson last Wednesday. Linda's ex-husband was around there. He was arguing with Linda.

Investigators say the believe the crime is a "targeted situation."

"We do not feel that this is a random act at this point," Arnott said.
