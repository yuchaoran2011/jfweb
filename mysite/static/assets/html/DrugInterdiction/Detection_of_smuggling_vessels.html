Detection of smuggling vessels

Source: U.S. Dept. of State Bureau of International Narcotics and Law Enforcement Affairs

Successfully intercepting drug smugglers depends on first detecting the vessels.

Visual detection – possible only with a high likelihood during daylight hours. Range depends on factors like size of the waves, but is typically on a clear day between 3 and 6 nautical miles.

On-board radar – These have a standard range of about 25 nautical miles, but their ability to detect vessels at such a distance depends on the profile and size of the object.

Long-range tracking aircraft – These have the ability to detect vessels at ranges up to about 250 nautical miles. Limited in bad weather conditions.
