The US is looking to intercept shipments of drugs. Cocaine smugglers are leaving a variety of locations in South America, and landing at locations in Central America. The drug smugglers tend to reuse the same routes. Ideal locations to patrol when looking for smugglers are places where these known smuggling routes converge. The majority of drug smugglers are operating using routes that follow the coast, or routes that are a straight line between the starting port and the destination, using go-fast speed boats. They do utilize other routes using other types of vessels, but with lower overall frequency.

This close to the equator, the Coast Guard vessels have approximately 12 hours of daylight during which to most effectively search for, intercept, and board smuggler's vessels.

Conversions for speeds and distances at sea:

  • 10 knots = 11.5 miles per hour
  • 10 nautical miles = 11.5 miles

    • Your job is to recommend action for where to deploy one of three patrol ships (descriptions below) to best help intercept shipments of cocaine. You can only deploy a single ship. The vessel you select will be deployed for 2 days in this operation.
